## Spot the red flags. Protect your peace.

**Love or Control?** is an AI-powered mobile safety and psychoeducational platform designed to help young people identify early signs of psychological, emotional, and digital violence in dating relationships before they escalate.

The project combines artificial intelligence, trauma-informed psychoeducation, privacy-first design, and ethical Web3 tools to help users recognize when affection becomes control.

---

## Project Overview

Dating violence rarely begins with visible aggression. It often starts with subtle behaviors disguised as love, jealousy, protection, or emotional intensity.

**Love or Control?** helps users answer one essential question:

> Is this love, or is this control?

The app allows users to enter a message, screenshot, or relationship situation and receive a clear, non-judgmental analysis through a red flag detection system.

The goal is not to diagnose, replace therapy, provide legal advice, or act as an emergency service.  
The goal is to provide clarity, psychoeducation, privacy, and safer next steps.

---

## The Problem

Young people often struggle to identify non-physical forms of violence in dating relationships.

Common patterns include:

- Digital surveillance.
- Excessive jealousy.
- Pressure to share passwords.
- Demands for live location.
- Emotional manipulation.
- Gaslighting.
- Isolation from friends or family.
- Sexual pressure.
- Threats disguised as love.
- Control over clothing, time, money, or social media.

These behaviors are often normalized as:

- “They are just jealous because they care.”
- “They only ask for my location because they worry.”
- “Couples should not have secrets.”
- “If I really loved them, I would answer immediately.”

But healthy love does not require fear, surveillance, guilt, or loss of autonomy.

---

## The Solution

**Love or Control?** is a private AI companion that helps users understand confusing relationship dynamics.

Users can submit:

- A message they received.
- A screenshot of a conversation.
- A description of a situation.
- A repeated behavior they are unsure about.
- A question such as: “Is this normal?” or “Am I exaggerating?”

The app responds with:

1. An alert level.
2. A short explanation of the behavior.
3. A possible psychological or digital violence pattern.
4. A green flag comparison.
5. A safe suggested response.
6. Recommended next steps.
7. Support resources when needed.

---

## Core Features

### Red Flags Scanner

The AI-powered scanner analyzes phrases, screenshots, or situations and identifies possible patterns of control or violence.

Detected patterns may include:

- Coercive control.
- Digital surveillance.
- Emotional manipulation.
- Gaslighting.
- Isolation.
- Jealousy disguised as care.
- Sexual coercion.
- Threats.
- Guilt-tripping.
- Password or location control.
- Non-consensual sharing of intimate content.
- Pressure to send intimate images.
- Sextortion risks.

The scanner does not label a person as abusive based on one phrase.  
It identifies patterns, explains risk levels, and provides guidance.

---

### Alert System

The app uses a simple color-based risk system:

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Green | Healthy or respectful behavior |
| Yellow | Mild concern or boundary issue |
| Orange | Possible pattern of control or manipulation |
| Red | High-risk behavior requiring support or safety planning |

---

### Green Flags Comparison

For every red flag, the app shows what a healthy version of that behavior could look like.

Example:

**Red Flag**

> “Send me your live location or I’ll get mad.”

**Green Flag**

> “Text me when you arrive if you feel comfortable. I just want to know you’re okay.”

This helps users distinguish care from control.

---

### Safe Replies

The app suggests possible responses that help users set boundaries without escalating the situation.

Examples:

```text
I’m not comfortable sharing my location all the time.
```

```text
I care about this relationship, but I also need space for my friendships.
```

```text
I’m open to talking, but not if I’m being pressured.
```

```text
I won’t share my passwords. Privacy is part of trust.
```

Users can copy, edit, or save these responses.

---

### Guardian Mode

Guardian Mode allows users to connect with a trusted contact.

Possible actions include:

- Sending a predefined safety message.
- Sharing a check-in.
- Saving a high-risk incident.
- Accessing quick support options.
- Activating a discreet safety flow.

Guardian Mode is user-controlled.  
The app does not automatically share private conversations.

---

### Evidence Vault

The Evidence Vault allows users to privately save relevant information.

Users may store:

- Screenshots.
- Notes.
- Dates.
- Descriptions of incidents.
- Voice notes.
- AI-generated risk level.
- Personal reflections.
- Support resources accessed.

The Evidence Vault is designed to help users organize their reality, especially in situations involving gaslighting, fear, confusion, or emotional manipulation.

---

### Escalation Map

The app helps users identify whether isolated behaviors are becoming a pattern.

Example escalation path:

1. Jealous comments.
2. Demands for immediate replies.
3. Requests for passwords.
4. Location tracking.
5. Isolation from friends.
6. Threats or intimidation.
7. Severe psychological, sexual, or physical risk.

The escalation map helps users recognize patterns earlier.

---

### Emotional First Aid

When a high-risk result appears, the app offers grounding and stabilization tools.

Examples:

- Take a breath before responding.
- Move to a safer place if possible.
- Contact someone you trust.
- You do not have to solve this conversation right now.
- Confusion is common when control is mixed with affection.

The app is designed to be trauma-informed, not alarmist.

---

### Community Support Space

The platform includes a safe, anonymous, moderated community space where users can share experiences and receive validation.

This space is designed for:

- Peer support.
- Psychoeducation.
- Emotional validation.
- Access to support resources.
- Prevention of revictimization.
- Anonymous participation.
- Gender-sensitive moderation.

The community does not replace therapy, legal advice, or emergency services.

## AI Layer

The AI system includes:

- Natural Language Processing.
- Pattern classification.
- Context-aware interpretation.
- Risk-level detection.
- Safe response generation.
- Psychoeducational feedback.
- Localized language understanding.
- Spanish, English, and regional slang adaptation.

The AI does not diagnose.  
It identifies possible communication patterns and explains them clearly.

---

## Privacy Layer

The app prioritizes:

- Local-first processing whenever possible.
- User-controlled input.
- No automatic monitoring of private chats.
- No sale of personal data.
- No sensitive data stored without consent.
- Clear deletion options.
- Anonymous or pseudonymous use.
- Minimal data collection.

The user should never feel watched by an app designed to protect them from surveillance.

---

## Solana Mobile / Web3 Layer

Solana Mobile is used ethically and selectively.

Possible integrations include:

### Pseudonymous Access

Users may access certain features without exposing their full identity.

### Proof of Care Badges

Users can receive non-sensitive educational badges after completing modules on:

- Consent.
- Healthy relationships.
- Digital safety.
- Boundaries.
- Emotional manipulation.
- Support-seeking.

### Evidence Integrity Hashes

Users may generate optional cryptographic hashes to verify that a file or note existed at a specific time.

The sensitive content itself is never stored on-chain.

### Privacy-Preserving Student Verification

Future versions may allow students to verify eligibility for university-specific resources without revealing unnecessary personal information.

---

## What We Do Not Store On-Chain

The app does not store the following on blockchain:

- Chat content.
- Screenshots.
- Personal stories.
- Names.
- Locations.
- Risk scores.
- Violence reports.
- Contact information.
- Counseling data.
- Therapy-related information.

Blockchain is used for trust and integrity, not exposure.
